“My bet is that standard deviation makes students shiver with fear and cringe with distaste more than any other two-word term, perhaps even long after they have completed their statistics course.”

Allan Rossman was born in Pennsylvania and grew up in Ellwood City. He went to college at Geneva College in Beaver Falls and received his Ph.D. in Statistics from Carnegie Mellon University. He taught at Dickinson College before moving to California where he has taught statistics at Cal Poly SLO since 2001. He became the department chair in 2013. The classes he has taught include introductory statistics, probability, simulation, and mathematical statistics. Some contributions to the field of statistics include various articles and textbooks to help teach statistics to students. He has also spoken at many conferences and given many presentations on teaching statistics. His most famous books are the Workshop Statistics series which goes over the fundamental concepts of statistics. Rossman has been the Chief Reader for the Advanced Placement program in Statistics and Program Chair for the US conference on Teaching Statistics and the Joint Statistical Meetings. Along with his co-author, Beth Chance, he has edited STATS: The Magazine for Students of Statistics. Rossman has published articles in the Journal of Statistics Education, the College Mathematics Journal, and the American Statistician. For his work, Rossman has been honored with the Fellow and Founders Award from the American Statistical Association (ASA), the MAA’s Haimo Award for Distinguished Teaching, and the ASA’s Waller Distinguished Teaching Career Award. Some other information about him is that he has a wife, Eileen, he has 3 cats, he loves mystery novels, and he plays fantasy sports. His fantasy sports team’s name is “Domestic Shorthairs”.
